Overview

Math 8 is designed around the BC curriculum’s Big Ideas, focusing on understanding numbers, patterns, relationships, and spatial reasoning. Students develop number sense by working flexibly with rational numbers and solving problems in multiple ways. Algebraic thinking is built through representing, analyzing, and generalizing patterns and relationships. Geometry concepts emphasize understanding and describing the relationships between objects and shapes in the world around us. Throughout the course, students are encouraged to explain their thinking, make connections, and apply mathematical reasoning to real-world situations. The goal is to build confident, thoughtful problem solvers who understand how and why mathematics works.

Units of Study

Order + timing (approx). Spring Break is not counted in the week ranges.

Unit Focus Weeks
U1 Financial foundations + number fluency (estimation, multi-step money, integers) (1–3)
U2 Ratios, rates, and unit pricing (best deals, comparisons) (4–6)
U3 Percent in real life (discounts, tax, tips, percent change) (7–9)
U4 Algebra as a tool (expressions, equations, modeling) (10–12)
U5 Measurement + geometry + data wrap (design + cost per unit) (13–16)

How this course works

  • Daily routine: warm-up → task time → practice → exit check
  • Choice levels: Green / Blue / Black (you can move between levels)
  • Real-world focus: money, price per unit, decision-making, problem solving
